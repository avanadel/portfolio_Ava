The Problem

The cloud storage market is huge with multiple big-name competitors like Google Drive, Dropbox, etc. It is projected that by 2022, more than 72% of the global organizations will migrate to the cloud from on-premise data centers. But the market is still young and there is scope for a new entrant. The purpose of this project is to identify the right market for a new entrant and design a cloud storage service from scratch with relevant features for that market.

The Solution

A new competitor with innovative features still offers the potential for advantageous competition in the marketplace. How can I prove a new way for students to organize their personal and academic files without the added stress of user directed organization?

The Process

Research and Discovery

I needed to uncover the frequencey of usage among cloud storage platforms among users in addition to their current pain points, likeable qualities, and desired functions.

Key Takeaways:
  • Respondents were most frustrated with disorganization, use of multiple platforms, lack of storage space
  • 95% use cloud storage services
  • Travelers used mobile apps for real-time status updates, and estimating departure/arrival times.
  • Respondents use cloud storage forsharing (75%), accesibility (85%) , and backup/recovery (75%)

Competitive Analysis

Out of all the cloud storage services on the market, Google Drive is the most cost-effective, with many native apps for creating content, such as Docs, Forms, and Sheets. While Google Drive offers real time collaboration features, users have found the interface difficult to navigate. Dropbox is an excellent platform for businesses and integrates content from the web into the users respective cloud, but has fewer ways to create content than Google Docs and has come under fire for security concerns. Pinterest has a more visually-focused interface, but lacks in collaboration and organizational tools.

Personifying the User

Based on the competitive analysis and user survey, I identified high level persona characteristics of a new entrant through hypothetical archetypes of a potential user, Jared.

Goals:

  1. To upload and organize files.
  2. To maintain files needed for different projects in one place.
  3. Keep files from different project organized and available to others working on shared documents/spreadsheets, etc.

Conclusion

Going through this journey was a process and an extremely valuable experience for me as a designer. I learned a lot about the importance of each step of the process, especially the research-gathering phase. The research you do is the foundation of what you design, and the more information you gather, the stronger your foundation.

The most difficult part of this process for me was creating the high-fidelity mockups. I knew what feelings and messages I wanted to convey with my design, but I had trouble translating that into a tangible finished product. What I found was that it was easier to step away for a quick break and a mental refresh every so often, or look for inspiration in other places, instead of mulishly trying to push through it. Sourcing opinions from others with A/B testing and usability testing was also invaluable, and a good way to get back on track.

By being thorough in my background research and gathering the requisite information, I’ve positioned my product to fill a space in the current market. By going through several iterations of designing and collecting user reactions through multiple rounds of user testing, I’ve created an experience that is intuitive and easy for people to use. The care and attention I put into each step of the process ensures that Muse is the best solution for the problem. The MVP for this design addresses the main problem we set out to solve in the cloud storage space - Unintuitive design and frustrating navigability and organization. edulife delivers a clean and friendly UI for users within the academic space to save files, create documents, and share and collaborate on content. Through multiple rounds of A/B testing and over 150 screens of iterations, I could provide a better solution by focusing on the foundations of intuitive design such as visibility, feedback, affordance, expectation, efficiency.
