edulife

A cloud storage platform for the everyday student

UX Research | Branding | UI Design | Prototype

PROBLEM

With an ever increasing competitive cloud storage platform market, a new competitor with innovative features still offers the potential for advantageous competition in the marketplace. How can I prove a new way for students to organize their personal and academic files without the added stress of user directed organization?

SOLUTION

edulife, a cloud platform built for students, allows users, to organize, upload, create, and share their content while keeping personal and academic files organized separately, maintaining a true work-life balance.

RESEARCH & DISCOVERY

User Research

Via Google Forms, I surveyed 20 users about their experiences with current cloud storage applications.

Key Findings

Pain Points: current cloud storage application make it difficult to organize files, folders, and photos. 75% cite uploading files and file organization as the most important features when using storage apps. Frequency: Google Drive: 100% Dropbox: 60%, iCloud 45%

Competitive Analysis

To build a usable, minimum viable product, I conducted a competative analysis of three leading competitors in the cloud/online storage platform market: Google Drive, DropBox, and Pinterest. Google Drive is a leader in the cloud storage space due to its simplicity of use and multi-platform features.The dashboard however, can be difficult to manage with limited file sorting options and need for secure internet connection. Dropbox shares visual files (PNG, JPEG, etc.), prides itself on collaboration features and accessibility from any device but risks users switching to another application with more options in addition to privacy constraints, and much like Google Drive, leaves users desiring a platform with navigable file organizaion. Pinterest is an excellent platform for organization, storage of interests, and networking but is lacking in document sharing. Users are also faced with potential security risks since “pins” are not always sourced from secure websites."

Strategy

With that in mind, the cloud storage platform to be created needed to be geared towards a busy people-students who enjoy balancing hobbies outside academia. What if there was a cloud storage platform that preorganized files and folders for its users to minimize the frustration of self cloud organization?

Personas and User Stories

Personas

Jared is a 45 year old High School Media Arts Teacher in Boston, MA. On a given weekend, he is either editing student podcasts or filming independently at Boston Common. Much of his professional of videography overlaps with my hobbies. Sometimes his personal video files wind up in my curriculum files and the process of self organization without Google Drive’s help is often frustrating. He wants a platform that can minimize the stress.

Charlotte is a 24 year old law school student in her second year at the Brooklyn Law School. She is passionate about food, CrossFit, meal prepping, and public speaking. She loves maintaining a healthy lifestyle, bookmarking recipes, and workout tips to my DropBox while maintaining the organization of my law school semester notes. Especially during exams, it gets increasingly difficult for Charlotte to maintain storage of both lifestyles. She wants the three to coexist on her cloud in a way that does not further heighten her stress levels.”

User Testing & Iterations

Initial Designs

Updates

Upon conclusion of the tests: the following changes were made to the edulife brand

  • • Refine onboarding/log in processes
  • • Shrink icon pixel size
  • • Minimize promixity of CTA buttons

  • • Lower font size of logotype

THE FINAL PROTOTYPE: BRINGING IT ALL TOGETHER

To meet web accessibiliity guidelines, significant changes in color (specifically the ivory and violet on the onboarding and homepages were eliminated and replaced with a royal blue, which, as it turned out, further meets one of the core values of the edulife brand: sophistictaion. While call to action buttons were aligned in the same column in lo-fi mock ups, they did not create a sense of unity for users among both color scheme, proximity, and visual hierarchy. To create a friendly user interface with navigable functions, all call to action buttons were enlarged with added coloring. Specifically, the user dashboard now presents a navigable page for the user to perform actions such as “Upload” or “Create.” Seconly, onboarding pages did promote log in or sign up proccesses to users by having the menu on the left hand side of the page. The call to action buttons “Sign in here” and “Join edulife” did not stand out to users as actions; during user testing, they were overlooked when attempting to log in or sign up for an account and users expressed difficulty in performing the task.

FUTURE CONSIDERATIONS

    In its current state, edulife is a minimally viable procuct. If the project were to be continued I would implement the following:

    1. Conduct more user tests: to test the functionality of call to action buttons and web accessibility colors
    2. As mentioned in the previous section, the one of biggest challenges was maintaining consistency across UI elements and establishing patterns for user interaction and animations in the early stages in the design process. Making a list of how elements would interact with one another when conducting user tests would have served me better prior to prototyping
    3. Design all app processes early in the design process!
    4. Design a process for users to upload folders, I had users only test uploading files
    5. Offer sign up and create account options in the same page! Making two separate pages created confusion points for users when testing.
    6. Seek feedback constantly and aggressively, two user tests during lo-fi mock ups and two during hi-fi mockups did not suffice. I would have benefitted from testing up to four people while conducting multiple tests throughout the design process. Testing each process, each flow, each design decision, will help build the backbone of an efficient, visually appealing, usable product.
