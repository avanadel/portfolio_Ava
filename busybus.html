BusyBus

A mobile transit app screen that shows users when the next buses are arriving to their stop.

UX Research | UI Design | Frontend Coding

EXERCISE OVERVIEW:

Public transportation apps are not new but provided me with a wealth of opportunities to work with imperfect data that is crucial to the users of a given app or service. I was tasked with creating a moble app called BusyBus, an application opperated by the local transit system that serves thousands of commuters in a large city.

PROBLEM:

Transit officials have been receiving mass amounts of customer service complaints due to expansion of routes. The numerous bus routes that have been added to the schedule have the same stops at the same bus stop. Public transit riders are having much difficulty navigating the new system, wanting to know when the next bus is arriving and how long their ETA is.

SOLUTION:

Busy Bus is a minimum viable product (a single screen in this case). designed to help bus riders know what the next arriving buses are arriving is and how much time they have to get to the next bus stop.

View GitHub Prototype

DISCOVERY PHASE

User Research

The first thing I wanted to do was to determine whether an app would indeed solve this problem and used Google forms to conduct a survey. The results confirmed users would like using an app to track the arrival time of their bus, all that was needed for this basic MVP, but also learned of desired features for future versions of the app

Pain Points

Participants highlighted inconsistent transit times and inaccurate arrival times, with public transit regularly, expressing desire for more efficient communication and more vehicles for transportation.

View User Survey

Strategy Phase

Competitive Analysis

A thorough analysis and breakdown on two available competitors in the market helped me to determine the strengths and weaknesses of the competitors within the market. As a result, I came up with interesting ideas on how a new competitor, in this case, BusyBus, could enter the market.

Based on the analysis, I was able to answer “How a new competitor enter the marketplace?”.

User Stories

A list of high priority tasks has been created for the minimum viable product (MVP) design. The importance of each task is assigned by considering the participants responses percentage in the user survey.

As a user, I want to receive:

  1. 1) Real-time information on bus routes
  2. 2) 2. Bus operation hour
  3. 30 consistently updated delays/service alerts
  4. 4) The available bus routes

Paper Prototype

The prototype is designed in a way to cover five high priority tasks of the users specified in user stories. The user would like to be informed on what is the next arriving bus is and how much time they have to get to the bus stop.

Usability Test

The created three-screen paper prototype of the proposed application was tested with three potential users. Based on a usability test script , the paper prototype has been tested and modified based on the findings and feedbacks from the users.



Usability test finding for the initial paper prototype:


1. • Participants reported confusion that app was user friendly.


2. • Not all participants knew the map was interactive.


3. • Participants were unsure where back buttons were leading to.

Visual Design Iterations

HI-FI MOCKUPS: ROUND #1

Based on the usability test findings as well as the Bloc program experts evaluation, the following presents the visual design iterations over the project timeframe. Active lines are blue, rerouted lines are red, and inactive lines are displayed in a deactivated grey state to help differentiate the status of the bus lines.



DEVELOPMENT PHASE

After testing to confirm the high fidility mockup functionality, it was the time to develop the front-end platform. Using HTML, CSS, GitHub, Git Bash, and Atom, the main screen of Busy Bus has been developed. A scroll overflow and hover were added to improve map visibility while scrolling the list of arriving buses and respective arrival times.



THE FINAL PRODUCT

BusyBus is a solution for the problem of a transit system expansion that has led to a complexity of usage for the users of the transit system. This final prototype provides a simple and accurate way for riders to know arriving busses and commmute time.
