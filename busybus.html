BusyBus UX Design Excercise

A platform to help the bus riders a comfortable bus commute

Visual Design
Frontend

OVERVIEW:

The concept of public transit trackers isn’t new but it provided me a wealth of opportunities to work with imperfect data that is incredibly important to the users of a given app or service. Few things will make people run faster than discovering their bus is a mere 2 minutes away from the bus stop. In this exercise, I was tasked with helping to create a mobile app called BusyBus, an application operated by the local city transit system that serves thousands of commuters in a large city.

Roles UX Research, Visual Design, & Front-end Web Development


Deliverables User surveys, user personas, competitive analysis, user stories & flows, paper prototype, lo-fi/hi-f wireframes, user testing analysis


Duration 3 weeks


Tools Figma, Atom, Draw.io, Google Forms,


PROBLEM:

Transit officials have identified a problem they would like to solve. Due to expansion, numerous bus routes have been recently added and many of those routes stop at the same bus stop. Riders want to know what the next arriving bus is and how much time they have to get to the bus stop. Simply rushing to the stop when you see a bus coming no longer works because it might not be the bus the rider is expecting. Riders are currently complaining the most about the bus stop at Washington and State, which has seven bus lines serving the stop.

SOLUTION:

BusyBus is a mobile iOS application (a single screen in this case) to help the bus riders to know what the next arriving bus is and how much time they have to get to their respective bus stop.

01Discovery & Strategy Phase

Survey results confirmed users would like using an app to track the arrival time of their bus. 85.7% of the bus riders found the public transit mobile app beneficial for their daily commute. 85.7% of the participants use the buses for reduced traffic congestion and 71.4% of the participants use the buses for saving money.

Competivie Analysis

As a result of an analysis of two competitors within the market (Google Maps & Moovit), I came up with interesting ideas on how a new competitor, in this case, BusyBus, could enter the market.

To observe the comprehensive findings on SWOT analysis click here.

Market entry strategy

“How a new competitor enter the marketplace?”.




I. Fees:
Google Maps provides real time data to users for free, marking itself as a fierce competitor for new navigation apps.



II. Live Alert:
BusyBus should provide users with a variety of get-on/off alerts, arrival time update alert, etc. The users need to be provided with enable/disable features for the alerts in case they do not like receiving in app notficiations.



IV. A user-friendly app:
I recommend having a simple and user-friendly application. The users should have the ability to select the required features while installing the app.



V. Route type:
The users need to be aware of the available route options and make a choice when on the go, providing the users with more route sorting based on the quickest route vs. the cheapest available route or live-alert.
