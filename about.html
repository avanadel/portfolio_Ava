I completed my year of service with AmeriCorps craving an even richer experience, one where I could still serve others and best utilize my Sociology degree to integrate the ability to be both empathetic, observant of cognitive behavior and social relationships into the world of design.



Design has and continues to give me the ability to pursue both a creative and open-minded approach to all facets of my life, from the clothing I buy to the nutrition tracker I use, to choosing Doordash or Postmates. Design has empowered me to live critically and consciously, to collaborate effectively, to empathize deeply; it has irrevocably changed my view of the world; it has gifted me with the importance of realizing that progress is not linear but is always on an upward trajectory.



As a biracial woman born and raised in Brooklyn, I grew up witnessing how diverse societies allow us to be productive innovators. As designers and researchers, our designs cannot produce an effective user experience without empathy for all users. I seek to further foster a user base that rises above biases, removes complacency, and ethnic representation through the user experience.
